What is financial literacy?


Simply put, financial literacy is the understanding of various complex financial concepts such as profitable saving techniques, financial retirement planning, and managing debt as well as more everyday issues such as managing budgets, tracking spending, and financial independence.


As one of the founding principles of our organization, Students for Financial Literacy was created on the basis that all individuals deserve to pursue financial literacy, regardless of their background or socioeconomic status.


The true meaning of financial literacy depends on the individual. Each person has their own goals and ambitions; the purpose of financial literacy is to find ways to effectively make these desires a reality.


To provide an easy-to-follow framework for financial education, SFFL uses the High School Financial Planning Program (HSFPP) curriculum as a starting point. We focus first on the following areas:


-Money management

Includes budget managing, tracking spending, meeting financial goals, and overcoming financial obstacles!

-Borrowing

Learning to control personal credit to maximize opportunity as well as learning to manage debt effectively.

-Earning Power

Understanding financial opportunity and the importance of using personal assets.

-Investing

Includes investing in stocks, bonds, Exchange Traded Funds, and other methods to grow savings over time!

-Financial Services

Making sure to use financial services such as banks sensibly and understanding the fine print.

-Insurance

Learning how to protect personal property as well as safeguard the future!
